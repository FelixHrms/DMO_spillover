{\large \textbf{Mechanism sketch: US Treasury auction shocks and euro-area bonds}}\\[4pt]
\textit{For discussion. Estimates are first-pass, German-led specification.}

\vspace{0.8em}

\textbf{Chain}
\bit
\item Weak 10y auction ($z_t > 0$, revealed 19:00 CET, after the EA close): dealers and hedge funds absorb the surprise supply, financed overnight in USD repo.
\item Stress hits the financiers: collateral values fall while financing demand, margins and haircuts rise.
\item Euro-area banks are those financiers: USD cash lending against UST collateral, mostly bilateral to hedge funds (few EA banks clear at FICC).
\item Their balance-sheet constraint tightens; low-margin EUR specific-collateral market-making is rationed first.
\item Bonds relying on exposed banks' intermediation lose financing $\Rightarrow$ extra yield rise at the $t{+}1$ close, unwound within $\sim$2 trading days (price pressure, not repricing).
\eit

\textbf{Measure.} $s_k$ = gross USD-against-UST repo over total repo book (bank-month, lagged); $\mathit{Exp}_{b,t} = \sum_k w_{k,b,t-1}\, s_k$, with $w_{k,b,t-1}$ = bank $k$'s share of bond $b$'s repo positions at the $t{-}1$ close. Everything predetermined. $\mathrm{sd}(\mathit{Exp}) = 0.033$.

\textbf{Specification}
\beq
\Delta y_{b,t} \;=\; \beta\, z_{t-1} \!\times\! \mathit{Exp}_{b,t} \;+\; \gamma\, z_{t-1} \!\times\! \mathit{Dur}_{b,t-1} \;+\; \delta' X_{b,t-1} \;+\; \alpha_b \;+\; \tau_t \;+\; \varepsilon_{b,t}
\eeq
$\tau_t$ absorbs the aggregate shock response, so $\beta$ compares bonds within the same day; $z \times \mathit{Dur}$ keeps the duration clientele of USD-active dealers out of $\beta$. Current estimate: $\hat\beta = 0.67$ ($p = 0.001$).

\vspace{0.4em}

\textbf{Worked example} --- 1-sd weak auction ($z = \sigma_z = 9.3$), announced Tuesday 19:00 CET:
\bit
\item Wednesday close, common move: $0.16 \times 9.3 \approx 1.5$ bp on all euro yields (daily vol 3--5 bp), with $\sim$0.5 bp extra bear-steepening for 10y vs.\ 2y.
\item Bank $A$: $s_A = 0.15$ (hedge-fund financing franchise). Bank $B$: $s_B = 0.02$. Bund $X$ is 70\% $A$-intermediated, Bund $Y$ 70\% $B$, rest at the average $s = 0.05$:
$\mathit{Exp}_X = 0.12$, $\mathit{Exp}_Y = 0.03$.
\item Wednesday differential: $0.67 \times 9.3 \times (0.12 - 0.03) \approx 0.6$ bp --- Bund $X$ cheapens 0.6 bp relative to Bund $Y$, on top of the common 1.5 bp. For a 2.5-sd tail auction: $\approx 1.4$ bp.
\item Friday close: differential back to $\approx 0$ (local projections die out at $h \approx 2$).
\eit

\textbf{To discuss}
\bit
\item Sign conventions of $z$ and $z^{(2)}$ from the PCA loadings (assumed here: $z > 0$ = weak auction = US yields up).
\item Constraint story vs.\ ``global dealers reprice faster'': the discriminating evidence is quantities --- exposed banks' EUR repo volumes and conditions should deteriorate over $t$..$t{+}2$ (directly testable in SFTR); related: asymmetry weak vs.\ strong auctions, quarter-ends.
\item France: interaction flips sign (DE/ES/IT positive). Likely no within-FR dispersion in $\mathit{Exp}$ (GSIB concentration in OAT intermediation)? Pooled spec needs country$\times$date FE.
\eit
