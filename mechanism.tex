{\large \textbf{Mechanism: a numerical example}}\\[4pt]
\textit{Representative values; $\varphi$, the repo-financed share and $\lambda$ to be calibrated.}

\vspace{0.8em}

\textbf{Setup}
\bit
\item Bank $A$: $s_A = 15\%$ of its gross repo book is USD financing against UST collateral; Bank $B$: $s_B = 2\%$ (cross-bank dispersion in the data: sd $\approx 3$ pp)
\item Bund $X$ is intermediated 70\% by $A$, Bund $Y$ 70\% by $B$ $\;\Rightarrow\; \mathit{Exp}_X - \mathit{Exp}_Y = 0.7 \times (0.15 - 0.02) \approx 0.09$
\item Repo-financed stock $\approx 25\%$ of amount outstanding (SFTR)
\item Market elasticity $\lambda = 5$ bps per pp of outstanding traded (D'Amico and King 2013; Eser and Schwaab 2016; De Santis and Holm-Hadulla 2020)
\eit

\vspace{0.4em}

\textbf{Step 1} \quad \textit{a 1-sd weak auction ties up the exposed bank's balance sheet}

Margin calls and client financing demand force each bank to free up $\varphi = 10\%$ of its USD--UST footprint, cutting its EUR market-making book by
\beqno
\varphi \times s: \qquad A\!: \; 10\% \times 15\% = 1.5\%, \qquad B\!: \; 10\% \times 2\% = 0.2\%
\eeqno

\textbf{Step 2} \quad \textit{the rationing hits the two bonds asymmetrically}

Financing withdrawn from Bund $X$ relative to Bund $Y$:
\beqno
0.7 \times (1.5\% - 0.2\%) \;\approx\; 0.9\% \text{ of the repo stock} \;=\; 0.9\% \times 25\% \;\approx\; 0.23\% \text{ of the outstanding}
\eeqno

\textbf{Step 3} \quad \textit{withdrawn financing moves the yield}
\beqno
\Delta y_X - \Delta y_Y \;\approx\; \lambda \times 0.23 \;=\; 5 \times 0.23 \;\approx\; 1.1 \text{ bps}
\eeqno

\vspace{0.4em}

\textbf{Check against the estimate.} The regression gives $\hat\beta \times \sigma_z \times (\mathit{Exp}_X - \mathit{Exp}_Y) = 0.67 \times 9.3 \times 0.09 \approx 0.6$ bps for the same exposure gap $\Rightarrow$ the data imply $\varphi \approx 5\%$, a modest reallocation --- consistent with the local projections, which show the differential unwound within $\sim$2 trading days.
